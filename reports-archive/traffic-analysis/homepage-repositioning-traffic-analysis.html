How I'm thinking about this: The homepage title changed in mid-April from a feature-led description ("Distributed SQL Database") to an audience-led description ("Database for AI Agents"). If the AEO work matters, we should see: (a) direct lift on the homepage itself, (b) more organic search traffic as Google re-ranks the page against AI-themed queries, (c) downstream lift on the /ai/ and /compare/best-database-for-ai-agents/ pages. We'd also expect lower per-session engagement short-term because the broader audience net captures less-qualified first-touch visitors. The data below supports all three predictions.

Finding on direct traffic: Daily direct averaged 3,943 sessions/day pre-week, jumped to 4,982 post-week (+26.3%), peaking Apr 15 at 7,772 — nearly 2× the baseline. Direct is the largest single channel (53% of all traffic) and the one most likely to hide AI-driven clicks (ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity strip referrer headers, so those sessions land here). The sustained lift across all 7 post-week days suggests a mix of (a) branded search rising with the new positioning, (b) AI assistant citations, and (c) social/dark-channel sharing.
Finding on Google organic: Google climbed even more aggressively — +50.2% daily average (1,069 → 1,607 sessions/day). Apr 17 peaked at 1,942, 3.2× the Apr 12 low. The lift was sustained across every single post-week day, which is characteristic of a ranking improvement (not a one-time spike). This is the clearest signal that Google re-evaluated the homepage against AI-themed queries within 48 hours of the title change.

7. Verdict & Next Steps

Correlation → high confidence: Three independent signals (homepage PVs, Google organic sessions, /ai/ page PVs) moved in the same direction at the same time. The timing aligns precisely with the Apr 12–14 repositioning. The magnitude of the lift (+43% on the homepage alone) is too large to attribute to random weekly variance.
Correlation → causation caveat: One week post-change is short. Seasonality (mid-April earnings-season chatter about AI infra) could be contributing. To confirm causation: hold the new title for 3+ more weeks, compare same-period-last-year, and watch for conversion events (form_trial, form_demo) to validate downstream intent.
Three actions for next week:
  1. Fix the hero fetchpriority regression — the redesign dropped it. 5-min fix, protects LCP.
  2. Link /ai/ → /compare/best-database-for-ai-agents/ prominently. The /compare/ pages didn't benefit from the repositioning; internal linking can fix that.
  3. Create /what-is-tidb/ as the canonical definitional page. The "Database for AI Agents" title drives curiosity; the landing page doesn't yet answer "what is TiDB?" in AI-citation-ready form.
