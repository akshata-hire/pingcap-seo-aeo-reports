Executive Summary

5 key findings from correlating AEO improvements with GA4 traffic data
  1. Overall traffic declined -66% (W1 vs W5) despite AEO improvements — driven by a GTM tracking change on Mar 18 that broke Click_to_Cloud and form_trial event tracking, plus what appears to be seasonal/external factors.
  2. Blog traffic dropped -69% after article pruning (943→242), but per-article efficiency rose from 20.0 to 24.0 views/article (+20%). Quality over quantity.
  3. Engagement metrics improved significantly in Week 5 — bounce rate dropped to 69.3% (from 77.7%), session duration rose to 151.2s (from 83.7s). Correlation with AEO: r=0.644 (Moderate).
  4. Comparison page traffic remained stable at ~90/day despite pages growing 4→10. New pages are being indexed but haven't yet attracted significant organic traffic (typical 4-8 week Google indexing lag).
  5. /ai/ page showed a traffic spike (208 PV on Apr 8, 3x normal) coinciding with the hero image WebP + fetchpriority fix — improved page load likely improved crawlability and user experience.
  6. New content hubs (/developers/, listicle comparisons) show early traction. The developer hub averages ~8 PV/day with a launch spike of 101 PV on Mar 12. Listicle-style comparison pages (/compare/best-distributed-sql-databases/, best-database-for-ai-agents, best-databases-for-saas-applications-at-scale) add long-tail visibility beyond head-to-head comparisons.

8. Methodology & Caveats

Data Sources: Google Analytics 4 (GA4) via MCP integration. All data pulled on April 12, 2026.

Date Range: March 9 - April 12, 2026 (35 days, 5 weeks).

AEO Scores: From weekly SEO/AEO audit reports conducted on pingcap.com.

Statistical Method: Pearson product-moment correlation coefficient. N=5 (weekly aggregates).

Limitations:

  • N=5 is insufficient for statistical significance. All correlations are directional indicators only.
  • Correlation does not imply causation. Traffic changes may be due to seasonality, marketing campaigns, algorithm updates, or other factors.
  • GTM tracking disruption on Mar 18 renders conversion data unreliable for the full period.
  • SEO changes typically take 4-8 weeks to fully impact organic traffic. This 5-week window may be too short for many improvements.
  • Timezone: GA4 data uses Asia/Shanghai timezone.
